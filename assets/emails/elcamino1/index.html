El Camino Health’s
Respiratory Therapists Fight Back
Against COVID-19


Our Respiratory Therapy (RT) team has been fighting against this virus from the day the first patient arrived, and there hasn’t been a break in the work since. They engage with every patient exhibiting respiratory distress and follow their care until they are discharged from the hospital. The work is difficult, the hours long and the emotional toll high. They are adapting to ever‑changing protocols to protect their safety and the safety of their patients, often offering emotional support to patients who are suffering, afraid and isolated from loved ones at a very scary time. Whether picking up extra shifts, bringing food to share or offering encouragement, our RT team has rallied together to vigorously battle this virus each and every day.

Stories of Gratitude
Community Support from Coast to Coast


Virginia resident Laura Davis was looking for ways to help during COVID-19, so she reached out to lifelong friend Tara Connor, a nurse at El Camino Health. The answer she received was “encouragement.” In response, Davis sent a care package of 80 bags of candy and 12 jars filled with positive messages to support the staff at El Camino Health. “It is very cool to see the community come together like this and give back to the healthcare providers. Hearing ‘wow, this touched us’ is really nice too,” said Davis.

In addition to El Camino Health, more than 84 jars were distributed to eight hospitals around Virginia Beach, VA. Davis collects donations via Amazon, Venmo and drop offs at her home. Follow the story at #JarsOfLoveFromVB.
