El Camino Health’s
Blue Angels Take Flight


When El Camino Health saw its first patients with COVID-19, hospital employees were there to answer the call. True to their nature, nurses and staff set their anxieties aside and put the needs of their patients first.

The first patients suspected of or confirmed with having COVID-19 received care from the teams in CCU and 3CW in Mountain View. As the patient numbers increased, 4A (Surgical/Pediatrics) served as overflow, since that unit could be converted to negative pressure air flow for isolation precautions.

Once patients arrived, nurses on 4A came forward to provide care, adapting to the situation, being resourceful when necessary, and having a positive attitude, earning them the title “Blue Angels.”

“Healthcare professionals are trained for situations like this,” said Molly Cyr, RN, clinical manager of the Surgical/Pediatric unit. “A number of nurses on 4A, and I would add all of 4A, and those who float from other units, really stepped up when needed. The entire team did a great job.”

As the hospital continues to care for COVID-19 patients, the need for care has quickly surpassed the size of this small group and depends on staff from throughout the hospital. These Blue Angels — and all hospital staff — have risen to the challenge in the fight against COVID-19.
