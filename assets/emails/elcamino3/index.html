Nutrition Services Helps
Co-Workers Access Discounted Essentials


It was a spark of an idea that quickly grew into a flame. Our nutrition services team wanted to help their colleagues by offering scarce grocery store items, like paper towel, toilet paper, disinfectant wipes and some essentials, for purchase inexpensively in the cafeterias at both campuses. Because of their thoughtfulness, tired employees and physicians working long hours don’t have to go shopping for hard-to-find items. Growing each day, the cafeterias now offer milk, eggs, fruits, vegetables, bread and other staples. The entire nutrition services team contributed to the effort and the response is overwhelming.

Volunteers Keep Giving…
From a Distance

Volunteering took on new meaning for El Camino Health in the wake of the coronavirus. Dedicated volunteers throughout the organization, who give their precious time and talent to support and bring joy to patients and staff at both hospital campuses, are still supporting the hospital remotely. Auxiliary volunteers who knit caps for newborns, sew pillows and do other work to support patients are still busy with many of those tasks, but recently, they showed appreciation for your hard work by bringing dozens of donuts to each hospital to be given out at every shift. The surprise delivery made everyone’s day just a bit brighter! El Camino Health Foundation Board members are also doing their part to support you by establishing the COVID-19 Emergency Response Fund and directing resources to projects that directly impact you, such as purchasing additional PPE and supporting the YMCA Kid’s Camp. Additionally, a core group of spiritual care volunteers pray daily for hospital staff. We thank all of our volunteers for their generosity and their efforts to show appreciation for your critical work.

During National Healthcare Volunteer Week (April 19-25), we salute volunteers everywhere for their special efforts during this pandemic. Learn more here.
